What is Quishing?

Quishing is a phishing attack that uses malicious QR codes to trick users into visiting fake websites, downloading malware, or revealing sensitive information. It's "QR code phishing" combined into one word.

üì±

Understanding Quishing

Quishing attacks exploit the trust users have in QR codes. Attackers place fake QR codes in public spaces or digitally to deceive victims.

  • Placed over legitimate codes
  • Posted on fake advertisements
  • Sent via email or SMS
  • Shared on social media
üö®

Attack Methods

Attackers use creative tactics to deploy quishing attacks:

  • Overlay legitimate codes with malicious ones
  • Place codes at parking meters & toll booths
  • Use restaurant menus with fake codes
  • Deploy codes in invoices and bills
  • Add codes to event posters
‚ö°

Potential Risks

Scanning malicious QR codes can lead to:

  • Credential theft and identity theft
  • Malware installation
  • Financial fraud
  • Data breach exposure
  • Unauthorized access to accounts
‚úÖ

Stay Safe

Follow these essential practices to protect yourself:

  • Verify QR code source and location
  • Check URL preview before opening
  • Use a QR code scanner with security features
  • Never enter passwords from QR redirects
  • Report suspicious codes

üõ°Ô∏è Essential Safety Tips

1

Inspect the Source

Check if the QR code looks legitimate. Is it properly placed? Does it appear to be printed or sticker-covered? Hackers often paste codes over legitimate ones.

2

Preview Before You Click

Most modern phones show a URL preview. Read it carefully before scanning. Does it look like an official website?

3

Never Login via QR

Don't enter passwords on sites accessed through random QR codes. If you must login, do it directly on the official website.

4

Verify the Domain

Check if the domain is legitimate. Look for HTTPS, proper spelling, and official branding. Beware of similar-looking domains.

5

Use Security Tools

Enable browser security features, use antivirus software, and keep your phone's OS updated to the latest version.

6

Trust Your Instincts

If something feels off about a QR code or where it's placed, don't scan it. Better safe than sorry!

üîê If You've Been Compromised

  1. Disconnect from the internet - Prevent further data leakage
  2. Change your passwords - Use strong, unique passwords for all accounts
  3. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) - Add an extra security layer
  4. Monitor your accounts - Check for unauthorized transactions
  5. Run malware scan - Use antivirus software to check your device
  6. Contact your bank/organizations - Alert them immediately if financial data was compromised
  7. Report to authorities - File a report with local law enforcement or cybercrime units
  8. Consider identity theft protection - Monitor your credit reports
